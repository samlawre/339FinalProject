Comparing Barcelona to London

Barcelona, Spain vs. London, England

Let's compare these two popular study abroad cities side-by-side.

Barcelona Cost of Living

While the cost of living in Barcelona is relatively high compared to the rest of Spain, compared to many European cities it costs significantly less to live and rent in Barcelona. According to Numbeo.com, you would need around 3,309.49£ (3,852.67€) in Barcelona to maintain the same standard of life that you can have with 5,100.00£ in London (assuming you rent in both cities).

London Cost of Living

The cost of living in London is significantly effected by the cities rent prices, as the average rent in London is 52% higher than the average rent in Barcelona. Other cost of living expenses like consumer prices, groceries, and restaurant prices are also between 16% - 35% high in London than Barcelona according to Numbeo.com.

Barcelona Transportation

Transportation in Barcelona and London are very comparable in terms of both cities having strong public transportation systems and being highly walkable cities. The metro (underground train system), trams, and busses are some of the most common and cost-effective ways to get around Barcelona. The average cost is €2.40 for a single metro or bus ticket.

London Transportation

Transportation in London is similarly accessible and barely more costly as the average cost for a single ticket to use the underground (metro) is only £2.50. The average monthly cost in that London communiter pay is also £186 pounds, spent across trains, cabs, etc.

Barcelona Safety

Safety in Barcelona and London are comparable according to Numbeo.com as the Barcelona and London crime index are similarly moderate with the cities earning scores of 50 and 53 out of 100, respectively. As the second largest city in Spain and a large tourist destination, pickpocketing and petty crime can be a concern but serious crime is very rare. Barcelona police forces (Guardia Urbana de Barcelona) is reported to be effective and can be reached at any time by calling the number 092.

London Safety

London safety is comparable to Barcelona as a big city and large tourist destination, the London Police keep the city center quite safe but the area also similar suffers from petty crime. Numbeo's Safety scale shows that London and Barcelona safety is very similar as both cities earned moderate safety ratings in the last 10 years.

Barcelona Food

Spanish tapas, traditional Catalan cuisine, and innovative modern cooking make Barcelona one of Europe's most appealing destinations for foodies. Barcelona is known for seafood paella dishes (see image below), croquettes, jamon iberico, and many more great spanish dishes. Average meal prices in a restaurant range from 7 to 20 Euros, making it a very budget friendly destination for food and restaurant outtings.

London Food

London has an outstanding culinary scene with thousands of restaurants and markets in neighborhoods throughout the city. Food's London is famous for include the English Breakfast, Bangers and Mash, fish and chips, and roast dinners (see image below). London is a very diverse city with a large portion of its residents (up to 55%) having not been born in England, and the food scene in the city reflects this diversity with a host of delicious restaurants and culinary styles at all price points. Average meal prices in a restaurant range from 12 to 20 Euros, only slightly more expensive than Barcelona.

Barcelona Night Life

In terms of night life, Barcelona is one of the world's best party cities. With rocking clubs, jam-packed bars, late night beach clubs, and regular street fiestas and music festivals (especially during summer). What's more, many sources say it's much cheaper to party in Barcelona than London and New York, and the scene is easier to navigate. Port Olimpic serves as an unofficial nightlife strip, with fancy beach lounge bars and restaurants that turn into clubs at night. A more bohemian alternative is Plaça Reial in the Gothic Quarter, with r'n'b, jazz and rock clubs, and plenty of cool bars.

London Night Life

Night Life in London is very comparable to Barcelona. As one of the biggest cities in the world, London is the place to be if your looking for a diverse and interesting night life scene. From Leicester Square to Shoreditch, there's always something going on, and most places have a lively after-hours scene. If you're looking to dance, check out one of the many clubs in Soho or Mayfair. Bars and clubs are typically more expensive in London than Barcelona, however.
